**6. Important Locations and Objects:**

*   **The Submerged City of Moonhaven:**
    *   **Detailed Description:** A vast, shattered elven megacity, mostly submerged under unnaturally drawn-in water. The architecture is elegant, elven, and nature-themed, with districts named after trees (Mithilorn, Silphane, Larendel, Calathiel). The lower levels are demolished ruins, while the upper tiers are more intact but overgrown with kelp and algae. The water is dark and murky, with shafts of light piercing the gloom. Small fish huddle in ruined buildings, avoiding the dead open water.
    *   **Function and Purpose:** Former capital city of the North Dome. Now, it's the site of the party's investigation into the dome's collapse and the source of the magical water draw.
    *   **Narrative Significance:** It is the primary setting and objective of the current story arc. It is also the place Faye Lara told Kaladen he would "find who you are," making it central to his personal quest. The city's structure (tiered levels, holy district) provides clues to its culture and the potential location of the magical wellspring.

*   **The Spider-Web Air Tunnels:**
    *   **Detailed Description:** An intricate, city-spanning network of tunnels constructed from thick, sticky spider silk, suspended underwater. The tunnels are filled with breathable air and illuminated by the soft, eerie glow of bioluminescent fungi and plants (identified as Feywild-native) whose roots are woven into the silk. The tunnels vibrate slightly with the party's weight.
    *   **Function and Purpose:** A highway and nest for a massive spider colony, used to transport air from the surface and expand their territory. For the party, it serves as a safe passage through the flooded city, allowing them to breathe and bypass threats.
    *   **Narrative Significance:** A unique and creative solution to the party's environmental challenge (drowning). It introduces a new, mysterious faction (the spider colony) and provides a tense, claustrophobic setting for their stealthy travel. The Feywild plants hint at the deep fey influence in the region.

*   **The Prophetic Mural of Calathiel:**
    *   **Detailed Description:** A massive, 50-foot wall of carvings located in a submerged building within the holy Calathiel district. It depicts a grand storyboard of elven history, featuring trees, figures, and the story of the goddess Jeya. One specific carving of an elven child has a hand pointing outwards, which pulses with a magical energy only Kaladen can perceive.
    *   **Function and Purpose:** An ancient historical record and a magically sealed repository of a hidden prophecy. It acts as a trigger for Kaladen's vision.
    *   **Narrative Significance:** The single most important object in the session. It serves as the vehicle for a massive lore exposition, revealing the origin of the Induven, the role of Jeya, and the prophecy of the Fae King. It directly ties Kaladen's personal quest to the history of the entire continent.

*   **Waterlogged Magic Scroll:**
